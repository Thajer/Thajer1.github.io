Kerala's beaches, backwaters, lakes, mountain ranges, waterfalls, ancient ports,
palaces and religious institutions are major
attractions for both domestic and international
tourists. The major beaches are at Kovalam, Varkala,
Kozhikode, Fort Kochi, Cherai, Alappuzha,etc.
One of the popular hill station is Munnar.
Padmanabhaswamy Temple in the state
capital of Thiruvananthapuram holds the record as the
wealthiest place of worship in the world.

Tourist Places

Kovalam

Kovalam is famous for its beaches, among the
most unspoilt in India. It is also one of the oldest tourist
enclave in India. Kovalam has 3 major beaches. The
southernmost, known as the Lighthouse Beach is the most
popular of the three.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha is famous for its boat races, houseboat
holidays, beaches, marine products and coir industry.
Lower areas of the district, known as Kuttanad, is one of the
few places in the world where farming is done below sea level.
